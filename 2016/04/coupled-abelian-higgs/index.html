Coupled Abelian Higgs

Posted on Mon 11 April 2016

The Abelian Higgs model is perhaps the simplest example of spontaneous symmetry breaking which gives mass to the boson. The Lagrangian is given by

\begin{equation} \mathscr{L} = - \frac{1}{4} F_{\mu\nu}^{2} + | \partial_{\mu} \phi + i e A_{\mu} \phi | + m^{2} |\phi|^2 - \lambda |\phi|^{4}; \end{equation}

however, I am interested in the next simplest scenario which involves two Higgs charged under the same gauge symmetry. The Lagrangian becomes:

\begin{align} \mathscr{L} = - \frac{1}{4} F_{\mu\nu}^{2} + | \partial_{\mu} \phi_{1} + i e_{1} A_{\mu} \phi_{1} | + m_{1}^{2} |\phi_{1}|^2 - \lambda |\phi|^{4}; \end{align}
